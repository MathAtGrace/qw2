Theorem 3.7.6.

The probability of the complement of an event \(E\) is
\begin{equation*} P(\bar{E}) = 1 - P(E)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Proof.

Remember that \(P(E) = \frac{\card{E}}{\card{S}}\text{,}\) the number of outcomes in the event \(E\) divided by the total number of outcomes. But how many outcomes are not in \(E\text{?}\) All the others. That is,
\begin{equation*} \card{\bar{E}} = \card{S} - \card{E}\text{.} \end{equation*}
So for sure, we have
\begin{equation*} P(\bar{E}) = \frac{\card{\bar{E}}}{\card{S}} = \frac{\card{S} - \card{E}}{\card{S}} = \frac{\card{S}}{\card{S}} - \frac{\card{E}}{\card{S}} = 1 - P(E)\text{.} \end{equation*}
